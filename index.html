KonaTech — Weekly Risk Review
Lesson 8 – Explain Vulnerability Management
Scenario

KonaTech’s SOC ingests weekly vulnerability rollups that merge: (1) internal scanner results, (2) vendor SBOM deltas, and (3) threat intelligence (CTI) from both an open-source feed and a proprietary feed, plus ad-hoc OSINT checks (Shodan, forum chatter). This week’s bundle includes exploitable CVEs on customer-facing systems, a public cloud storage misconfiguration exposing PII, and aging EOL infrastructure isolated behind segmentation. Leadership priorities: • Minimize data exposure risk (cloud storage/PII). • Maintain uptime for customer-facing services (tight maintenance windows). • Use compensating controls where patching isn’t immediately feasible. • Close items only after verification via re-scan. Your tasks: 1) Triage findings using exploit maturity, exposure, and business impact (guided by CTI/OSINT context). 2) Pick scan type (credentialed vs non-credentialed) and cadence per asset, considering maintenance windows and the need to verify fixes via re-scan.
